Discover the Origins of Our Coffee

Coffee is grown in many regions around the world, each with its unique flavor profile and characteristics. Here are some of the most renowned coffee origins:

1. Philippines

The Philippines has a rich coffee heritage, being one of the few countries that grows all four main coffee varieties: Arabica, Robusta, Excelsa, and Liberica (Barako). Barako coffee, known for its strong, bold flavor with hints of dark chocolate and anise, is a local favorite. The country's diverse climate, from the mountains of Benguet and Sagada to the farms of Batangas and Cavite, produces a wide range of coffee flavors.

2. United States

While the U.S. is mainly a coffee-consuming country, Hawaii and California are known for their coffee production. Hawaiian Kona coffee is highly prized for its smooth, mild flavor with hints of honey, nuts, and fruit. Meanwhile, California has recently started producing high-quality coffee in regions like Santa Barbara and San Diego. These U.S. coffee-growing areas benefit from volcanic soil and favorable climates, leading to a distinct and premium coffee experience.

3. Brazil

Brazil is the largest coffee producer in the world, known for its rich, chocolatey flavors and low acidity. Brazilian coffee often has a nutty or sweet profile, making it a popular choice for espresso blends.

4. Costa Rica

Costa Rican coffee is known for its bright acidity and full-bodied flavor. The country's strict coffee regulations ensure high-quality beans, often with fruity and citrusy notes.

5. Jamaica

Jamaican Blue Mountain coffee is one of the most sought-after coffees in the world. It is known for its mild flavor, smooth body, and lack of bitterness. The unique growing conditions in the Blue Mountains contribute to its exceptional quality.
